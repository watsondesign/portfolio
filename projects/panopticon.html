panopticon

An immersive experience constructed around my senior capstone thesis on electronic data collection and mass surveillance. Users stepped in front of a 10-foot wide projection where their silhouette was periodically interpreted by an X-box Kinect. Vinyl instructions ahered to the floor instructed guests to connect to a Wi-Fi network. When a user connected, their device's name was projected over their body. A stream of live data was paired to each device and flowed to its mirrored location on screen. Up to 6 people could participate.

It was incredibly difficult to build with only 18 days to execute. I began by learning Touchdesigner and experimenting with visuals. Then, I researched how data is sent across the Wi-Fi network: how it could be captured and stored in a database. Finally, I wrote a program that continuously monitored the network and sent the data to Touchdesigner when the user connected.
